## Why
I'm a manager at ALDI. I rarely build the artefacts I depend on — I commission,
review, correct and present them. My day is trends and risks in data someone else
prepared, submissions for GDM and senior audiences, a heavy flow of meetings, and
email that has to be clear the first time. I want enough Word fluency to review
work fast and spot what's wrong, do the small jobs myself without waiting, and use
that fluency as a launchpad into GenAI productivity.

## Success looks like
- Produce submissions and papers with the structure senior readers expect — and reuse it.
- Review long documents fast: navigation, tracked changes, comments that direct rework.
- Keep version control of documents sane: one master, clean revisions, no _final_v7.
- Make a document's numbering, TOC and cross-references trustworthy before it leaves my desk.
- Do the same document work with AND without AI — then pivot it into GenAI tools for speed.

## Constraints
- Full-time role: lessons must be short and immediately useful (single tangible win each).
- Enterprise GenAI available: Aldi:GPT, Microsoft Copilot (O365), GitHub Copilot.
- Windows + desktop Word.
