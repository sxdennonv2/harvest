## Why
I'm a manager at ALDI. I rarely build the artefacts I depend on — I commission,
review, correct and present them. My day is trends and risks in data someone else
prepared, submissions for GDM and senior audiences, a heavy flow of meetings, and
email that has to be clear the first time. I want enough PowerPoint fluency to review
work fast and spot what's wrong, do the small jobs myself without waiting, and use
that fluency as a launchpad into GenAI productivity.

## Success looks like
- Build and fix the decks I present upward: message-first slides that survive a GDM audience.
- Review a team deck fast: spot the buried message, the chart that misleads, the slide doing two jobs.
- Keep a consistent, credible look without hand-formatting every slide.
- Cut a 20-slide draft to the 8 that carry the decision.
- Do the same deck work with AND without AI — then pivot it into GenAI tools for speed.

- Full-time role: lessons must be short and immediately useful (single tangible win each).
