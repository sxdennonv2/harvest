## Why
I'm a manager at ALDI. I rarely build the artefacts I depend on — I commission,
review, correct and present them. My day is trends and risks in data someone else
prepared, submissions for GDM and senior audiences, a heavy flow of meetings, and
email that has to be clear the first time. I want enough Excel fluency to review
work fast and spot what's wrong, do the small jobs myself without waiting, and use
that fluency as a launchpad into GenAI productivity.

## Success looks like
- Read an unfamiliar workbook fast: find the logic, judge whether the numbers can be trusted.
- Spot the classic risks (fragile ranges, pasted values, silent errors) in work I'm reviewing.
- Do the small analytical jobs myself — a quick summary, a sanity-check pivot — without waiting.
- Ask for the right thing: know what a well-built workbook looks like so I can commission one.
- Review the same work with AND without AI — then pivot it into GenAI tools for speed.

- Full-time role: lessons must be short and immediately useful (single tangible win each).

- Windows + desktop Excel.
