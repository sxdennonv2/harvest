Excel to a High Standard · Lesson 0006

Get & Transform: clean once, refresh forever

Tied to your mission: the same export lands every week, and every week you delete the junk rows, fix the headers, trim the text and change the types by hand. Get & Transform (Power Query) records those steps once, then replays them on the next file with a single Refresh. It is the difference between cleaning data and building a cleaning machine.

Practice workbook · open this first

Open the course workbook and click the Power Query tab — a deliberately messy raw export (a title row, stray spaces, mixed casing, a blank row) sits ready for you to import and clean. The Power Query soln tab shows the tidy result to aim for.

The one idea

Power Query is a recorder for data clean-up. Select the messy range, then Data > From Table/Range to open the Power Query Editor. Each fix you click — remove top rows, promote the first row to headers, trim and clean text, change a column's type, remove blank rows — is saved as a step in the Applied Steps list on the right.

When you Close & Load, the cleaned table lands back on a sheet. Next week, drop the new export in, hit Refresh, and every step runs again in order — no re-doing the work. The steps are recorded in a language called M, but you rarely touch it; the ribbon writes it for you.

Why it matters to an office assistant

A weekly export you clean by hand is exactly the kind of chore that eats your day and quietly goes wrong — a junk row missed, a column left as text so a total silently breaks, and no record of what you actually did. Everything you produce for the department carries its name, so a result that's clean but not reproducible is still a risk. A query does the same clean-up identically every time and writes down exactly what happened, so a report, tracker or minutes pack you hand off is trustworthy without you re-checking it line by line. It turns a recurring chore into a button — one refresh instead of another twenty minutes of copy-paste-delete.

The bad habit this undoes Re-cleaning the same export by hand every week — slow, and never quite the same twice, so the numbers drift and no one can say what was changed. Record the clean-up once and refresh it.

How to do it

On the Power Query tab, click any cell in the messy block and choose Data > From Table/Range. In the editor: use Remove Rows > Remove Top Rows to drop the title row, then Use First Row as Headers. Select the text columns and use Transform > Format > Trim (and Clean) to strip stray spaces. Set each column's type (a number column to Whole Number or Decimal). Use Remove Rows > Remove Blank Rows. Then Home > Close & Load. Compare your result to the Power Query soln tab.

Builds on text formulas: Trim and Clean here are the point-and-click cousins of the TRIM you wrote earlier — same fix, now recorded as a repeatable step.
