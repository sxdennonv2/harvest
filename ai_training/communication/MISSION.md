# Mission: Communication to a High Standard

## Why
We write all day — to suppliers, to directors, to each other — and the survey says it
plainly: the single most-wanted AI help in this office is *rewording*. Reword, rephrase,
formalise, tighten, fix the tone for senior leadership, remove the ambiguity for external
stakeholders. This course teaches the craft underneath those requests, so the machine's
rewrite is something you can judge, not something you must trust.

## Who it's for
The whole office — analysts, assistants, managers. (Wider than the functionality
courses' buying-analyst persona; the reword demand comes from every role in the survey.)

## Success looks like
- A draft that says exactly one thing, to a named reader, in a chosen register.
- Tone selected before writing and audited after — not sanded off in five rereads.
- Every revision keeps every fact, every ask, and every owner from the original.
- The same task done with AND without AI — then handed up the ladder with a check at
  every rung.

## Constraints
- Lessons must be short and immediately useful (single tangible win each).
- Enterprise GenAI available: Aldi:GPT, Microsoft 365 Copilot, GitHub Copilot.
- Context today is email-first; document, slides and chat contexts come later.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Long-form document design, presentations as a craft (PowerPoint course territory).
- Anything app-mechanical (that's the functionality pipeline).
