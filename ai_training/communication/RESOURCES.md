# Communication to a High Standard — Resources

Curated, high-trust sources. Knowledge for lessons is drawn from here, not from guesses.

> Note: links are the canonical, long-stable URLs for each source. Verify/trim on next
> online session (same seeding convention as the Excel course).

## Knowledge

- [plainlanguage.gov — Federal Plain Language Guidelines](https://www.plainlanguage.gov/guidelines/)
  The canonical plain-language reference: audience-first writing, active voice, short
  sentences, word choice. Use for: clarity & conciseness, structure, cutting hedges.
- [Purdue OWL — Professional, Technical Writing](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/subject_specific_writing/professional_technical_writing/index.html)
  Authoritative academic writing lab. Use for: email etiquette, tone and audience
  analysis, formal registers, memo/letter conventions.
- [Purdue OWL — Proofreading](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/proofreading/index.html)
  The writing-process proofreading section. Use for: sweep-based proofing strategies,
  why rereading fails, finding real-word errors spellcheck can't see.
- [GOV.UK — Content design: writing for GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/content-design/writing-for-gov-uk)
  The best operational style guide for concise public-facing prose. Use for: front-loading
  the point, plain words over formal ones, sentence-length discipline.
- [Nielsen Norman Group — How Users Read on the Web](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-users-read-on-the-web/)
  Evidence base for scanning behaviour. Use for: why the ask must be visible in the
  first two lines, formatting for skimmed email.

## Wisdom (books — cite by title, no link rot)

- **William Zinsser, *On Writing Well*** — clutter, simplicity, the audience contract.
- **Joseph M. Williams, *Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace*** — cohesion, emphasis,
  the craft of revision; the strongest source on *how* to revise, not just what good
  looks like.
