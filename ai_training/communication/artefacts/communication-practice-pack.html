Communication to a High Standard · Practice pack

The before/after pack

Three deliberately flabby drafts. Every lesson in this course revises one of them — by hand first, then up the GenAI ladder. The check never changes: the revision keeps every fact, every ask, and every owner from the original. Suppliers are fictional (shared with the Excel course).

Scenario A — The late price list

To: Marco Bianchi, Acme Foods (external supplier) · From: you · Situation: the Q3 price list was due last Monday; range review starts Thursday; second chase.

Must survive revision: Q3 price list is outstanding · second chase · updated case costs for snacks lines · confirm whether Stacked Chips 12pk 300g price changes · range review starts Thursday · owner of the next step: Marco.
Worked revision (peek after your own attempt)

Hi Marco, Following up on the Q3 price list — we haven't received it yet and range review starts Thursday, so it's now on the critical path. Could you send by Wednesday COB: 1. The Q3 list with updated case costs for the snacks lines 2. Confirmation of whether the Stacked Chips 12pk 300g price is changing — we're seeing conflicting figures If anything is blocking the list, tell me today and we'll work around it. Thanks, [Name]

Scenario B — The buried escalation

To: your director · From: you · Situation: you need a decision by Friday on accepting a 4.2% cost increase from Coastal Co, or the Cheddar 500g line delists in October.

Must survive revision: decision needed by Friday · 4.2% increase, Cheddar Cheese 500g, Coastal Co · rationale: milk input costs, partially supported by market data · three options (accept / push back / switch, each with a risk) · missing Friday = October slot lapses = Q4 delist · decision owner: the director.
Worked revision (peek after your own attempt)

Subject: Decision needed by Friday — Coastal Co +4.2% on Cheddar 500g Coastal Co have tabled a 4.2% increase on Cheddar Cheese 500g, citing milk input costs (partially supported by market data). If we don't respond by Friday, the October production slot lapses and the line delists for Q4. Options: 1. Accept — protects Q4 supply; sets a pricing precedent 2. Push back — some support in the data, but risks the Friday deadline 3. Alternative supplier — poor lead times; October slot unlikely to be met Recommendation: 2, with 1 as the fallback by Thursday noon. Can you confirm a direction by Friday 10am? Happy to talk it through before then.

Scenario C — The over-casual reply

To: Kim Tan, GreenField (external supplier) · From: you · Situation: replying to a query about promo volumes for Free-range Eggs 12pk; the answer is known but the draft reads like a text message.

Must survive revision: plan on 95 cases/week · promo fortnight scope · uplift possible but NOT committed · delivery day shifts Monday → Tuesday for that fortnight · warmth is fine, sloppiness is not.
Worked revision (peek after your own attempt)

Hi Kim, Promo volumes for Free-range Eggs 12pk: please plan on 95 cases/week for the promo fortnight. There may be upside depending on catalogue performance, but treat 95 as the committed planning number — I'll confirm any uplift as soon as we see the early sell-through. One logistics note: deliveries shift from Monday to Tuesday for that fortnight only. Thanks, [Name]

Scenario D — The promo thread

A four-message thread, Monday to Wednesday · Participants: you; Sam Lee, Harvest Lane (external supplier); Alex Moreau, Supply Chain (internal) · Situation: you're about to hand your desk over for two days and must summarise where this stands.

Must survive any summary: confirm +40 cases/wk (70 → 110) Rice Crackers 24pk with Sam by Friday — you owe it, slot lapses after · deliveries move Monday → Wednesday for the fortnight (DC condition; Sam has agreed) · open question: is Popcorn 10pk in the promo? — Alex is owed an answer; nobody has confirmed it · pallet configuration changes 1 September regardless — warehouse needs the heads-up.
Worked summary (peek after your own attempt)

Rice Crackers promo fortnight — where it stands: 1. Confirm +40 cases/wk (70 → 110) with Sam Lee by FRIDAY — production slot lapses after. (We owe Harvest Lane.) 2. If confirmed: deliveries move Monday → Wednesday for the fortnight — DC condition, Sam has agreed. 3. OPEN: is Popcorn 10pk in this promo? Alex needs an answer — nobody has confirmed either way. 4. Separate but real: Harvest Lane's pallet configuration changes 1 September regardless — warehouse needs a heads-up.
