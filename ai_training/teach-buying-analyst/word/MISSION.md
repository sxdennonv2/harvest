# Mission: Word to a High Standard

## Why
Submissions, training material, external documents — Word is where they're assembled,
reviewed and fought over. The survey's dominant Word demand is *wording*, which lives
in the communication course (document context); this course teaches the machinery that
makes a GDM submission assemble itself: styles, sections, tables that behave, track
changes without fear, templates. If a lesson would survive the app being swapped, it
isn't here.

## Success looks like
- Documents formatted by styles, never by hand — change the style, the document follows.
- A heading hierarchy that gives you the nav pane, the TOC and drag-reorganising free.
- Page numbering and sections that obey; tables that hold their shape across edits.
- Review rounds run through track changes and Compare, not filename archaeology.
- New documents born from the template, not from last month's copy.
- The same task done with AND without AI, handed up the ladder with a check per rung.

## Constraints
- Lessons short and immediately useful; single tangible win each.
- Enterprise GenAI: Aldi:GPT, Microsoft 365 Copilot, GitHub Copilot.
- Windows + desktop Word; a mock GDM submission stands in for the real thing.

## Out of scope (for now)
- The prose itself (communication course, document context).
- Mail merge; long-document publishing (indexes, cross-reference webs) until demanded.
