Slicers: swap the dropdowns for sliders

Tied to your mission: your PivotTable works, but the little grey Filter dropdowns are fiddly and hide what's actually selected. Slicers replace them with big, obvious buttons — a filter panel your director can read and click at a glance. Same pivot, a far better-looking, faster-to-drive report.

Practice workbook · open this first

Open the course workbook and use the PivotTable tab. Build the pivot from the PivotTables lesson first (Category in Rows, Cases and Revenue in Values) — this sub-lesson adds the slicers on top of it.

The one idea

A slicer is a filter you can see. Instead of a dropdown buried in the pivot, it is a floating panel of buttons — one per value — that filters the PivotTable when you click. A Timeline is the same idea for dates: a literal slider you drag across months or quarters. The pivot underneath is unchanged; the slicer is just a nicer set of controls wired to it, and one slicer can even drive several pivots at once.

Why it matters to a buyer

A report is used, not just read. When the filter is a row of labelled buttons, anyone can see that "Snacks" and "Q3" are selected and change it without hunting through menus — no training, no mis-clicks. It's the difference between a pivot only you can drive and a small dashboard your whole team can. That polish is exactly what makes a boss's pivot look "a level up".

The bad habit this undoes Leaning on the pivot's built-in Filters dropdowns — where the current selection is invisible until you open them, and no one but the author dares touch it. Slicers put the state on the surface, so the report explains itself.

How to do it

Click anywhere in the PivotTable, then PivotTable Analyze → Insert Slicer, tick Category and Supplier, and click OK. Two button panels appear — drag them beside the pivot. Click a button to filter; Ctrl-click for several; use the little clear filter icon (top-right of the slicer) to reset.

For dates, use PivotTable Analyze → Insert Timeline and pick Month — you get a slider you drag across the period. To make one slicer drive several pivots, right-click it → Report Connections and tick the pivots to link.

Try it for real

On your pivot, add slicers for Category and Supplier and a Timeline for Month. Click a category button and watch the totals move; drag the timeline to a single month. Then tidy the layout so the panels sit neatly beside the pivot — that arrangement is the report.
