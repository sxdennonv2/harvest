Excel to a High Standard · Lesson 0018

Flash Fill & Text to Columns: clean an extract

Tied to your mission: raw extracts arrive messy — names as "Surname, First", product codes mashed into one field. Flash Fill and Text to Columns split them apart in seconds, so you spend your time analysing clean data instead of retyping dirty data.

Practice workbook · open this first

Open the course workbook and click the Flash Fill tab — a "Surname, First" contact column sits on the left (with the first row already split as an example) and a mashed Product code column on the right, ready to break apart.

The one idea

Both tools split one column into several, but they learn differently. Flash Fill watches you do one row by example, spots the pattern, and fills the rest — press Ctrl+E. Text to Columns splits on a delimiter (a comma, a dash, a space) or at fixed widths, in one guided pass. Reach for Flash Fill when the pattern is easier to show than describe; reach for Text to Columns when there's a clean separator.

Why it matters to a buyer

The join and lookup skills from earlier lessons only work if the key columns are clean and consistent. A supplier field that's really "code-number-size" jammed together can't be grouped, filtered or matched until it's split. Cleaning is the unglamorous step that makes every later step possible — and doing it in seconds, not by hand, is what buys back your day.

The bad habit this undoes Retyping hundreds of split values by hand, or fighting a tangle of LEFT/RIGHT/MID formulas for a one-off clean-up. For a quick split you own, Flash Fill and Text to Columns are faster and clearer. (When the split must stay live as new data arrives, that's a formula or Power Query job — a later lesson.)

How to do it

Flash Fill. On the Flash Fill tab, C2 already shows the first name from the contact in A2. Click C3, start typing the next first name, and press Ctrl+E — Excel fills the column. Do the same for the last name in column D.

Text to Columns. Select the codes in F2:F6, then Data → Text to Columns → Delimited → Next, tick Other and type -, and Finish. The dept, number and size land in G, H and I.

Try it for real

Split both the names and the codes on the Flash Fill tab. Then sanity-check: do the first/last names read correctly for a tricky row, and did the code split into exactly three clean parts? Remember the difference — Flash Fill and Text to Columns produce static values, so if the source changes you re-run them; that's fine for a one-off clean.
