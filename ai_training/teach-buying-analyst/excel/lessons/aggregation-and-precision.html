Excel to a High Standard · Lesson 0008

Aggregation & precision

Tied to your mission: SUMIFS totals by criteria, but a summary needs more than totals — it needs how many lines sit behind a category and their average, both rounded so the report reads cleanly. COUNTIFS and AVERAGEIFS are the siblings of SUMIFS, and ROUND is the discipline that stops a stray 0.3333333 from making your report look unfinished.

Practice workbook · open this first

Open the course workbook and click the Aggregation tab — a product list with categories and revenue sits beside a small summary block waiting for a count and a rounded average per category.

The one idea

The ...IFS family all read the same way — a range to act on, then range/criteria pairs. COUNTIFS counts the matching rows: =COUNTIFS($A$2:$A$15, F2). AVERAGEIFS averages a value across them: =AVERAGEIFS($D$2:$D$15, $A$2:$A$15, F2) — note the value range comes first here, unlike SUMIFS' identical shape.

ROUND takes a number and a digit count: =ROUND(123.456, 0) gives 123, =ROUND(0.318, 3) gives 0.318. ROUNDUP and ROUNDDOWN force the direction when you need to always round a case count up. Rounding changes the stored value; number formatting only changes what's shown — use rounding when the number feeds another calculation.

Why it matters to a buyer

A total alone hides the shape of the range: two categories can share a revenue total while one is ten steady lines and the other is two spikes. A count and an average expose that. And a report peppered with long decimals reads as a draft — rounding to sensible precision is part of making numbers a director trusts at a glance.

The bad habit this undoes Filtering to a category and reading the status bar count, then typing it into the summary by hand — it's stale the moment the data changes. And leaving raw 0.33333 averages in a report because "the cell shows two decimals anyway". Compute the count and round the value.

How to do it

On the Aggregation tab the categories are listed in F2:F5. In G2 type =COUNTIFS($A$2:$A$15, F2) and fill down — each row shows how many lines that category has. In H2 type =ROUND(AVERAGEIFS($D$2:$D$15, $A$2:$A$15, F2), 0) and fill down for a clean whole-dollar average. The $ locks the data ranges so they don't slide as you fill.

Builds on SUMIFS & references: the criteria shape is identical to SUMIFS, and the absolute $ ranges are the same discipline — lock what should stay put, let the criteria cell walk down.
