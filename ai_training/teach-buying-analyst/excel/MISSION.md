## Why
I'm a buying analyst at ALDI working with numbers, formulas and calculations every day —
sourcing external data via SQL, then organising, summarising and surfacing the signals,
trends, risks and opportunities I present to my manager and director. I want to master Excel
properly (not the patchy, self-taught version I inherited), work the modern way, and use that
fluency as a launchpad into GenAI productivity.

## Success looks like
- Build robust, correct formulas with a sound mental model — no fragile ranges or retyping.
- Work the contemporary way: Tables, dynamic arrays, modern lookups, keyboard-first.
- Turn raw SQL extracts into clean summaries and analysis leadership can act on.
- Spot and undo inherited bad habits (and recognise them in others' workbooks).
- Do the same analytical task with AND without AI — then pivot it into GenAI tools for speed.
