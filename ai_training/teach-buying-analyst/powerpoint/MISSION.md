# Mission: PowerPoint to a High Standard

## Why
Decks are where the analysis meets the audience — and the survey's PPT demand
("refining PPT or training material", "formatting and templates", "preparing
presentations") is about decks that look right and travel well. This course teaches
the deck as an artifact: masters, alignment, the Excel seam, templates, the last mile.
What a slide *says* — the message, the headline — is the communication course's job
(slides context); if a lesson would survive the app being swapped, it isn't here.

## Success looks like
- Decks built on the master, so a change lands everywhere at once.
- Slides that are measurably tidy: aligned, distributed, one visual hierarchy.
- Data on slides that stays alive to its workbook — paste-link, never screenshot.
- New decks born from the corporate template, not fought into it.
- Presentations rehearsed with presenter view; exports that survive other machines.
- The same task done with AND without AI, handed up the ladder with a check per rung.

## Constraints
- Lessons short and immediately useful; single tangible win each.
- Enterprise GenAI: Aldi:GPT, Microsoft 365 Copilot, GitHub Copilot.
- Windows + desktop PowerPoint; a mock-corporate master stands in for the real one.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Slide messaging and narrative craft (communication course, slides context).
- Animation/transition work beyond the defaults; video production.
