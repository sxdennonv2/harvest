The deck pack

Scenarios for practising deck mechanics. Each describes its specimen precisely enough to reconstruct — and rung 4 of the relevant lessons builds the specimens from scratch, which is the point. Suppliers are fictional and shared with the course family. Each scenario states what a correct result must contain.

Scenario A — The untidy slide (arrange & align)

One slide from the monthly category review, assembled in a hurry.

Slide spec (reconstruct or imagine): · Title "Snacks — month in review" overlapping the logo, top-right · Three stat boxes: left one 2cm higher than the others, uneven gaps (1.1cm / 2.6cm) · Two fonts in play (the template's + a pasted Calibri survivor) · A fourth box, rotated 1°, nobody knows why
A correct tidy: boxes aligned on a common top edge and distributed with equal gaps (Align/Distribute, never nudging by eye) · elements that move together grouped · one font family — the template's · the title inside its placeholder, clear of the logo · zero rotations that aren't decisions.

Scenario B — The screenshot deck (charts & the Excel seam)

The monthly review deck carries four "charts" — all screenshots of the course workbook. The workbook's revenue figures changed yesterday. The deck didn't notice.

A correct rebuild: the revenue-by-category chart pasted as a linked chart from the workbook (Paste Special → link), so a workbook change updates the slide · one comparison per chart, gridlines light, no legend for a single series (the Excel course's chart discipline, now travelling) · the test: change a number in the workbook, watch the slide move · screenshots demoted to what they're for — pictures of things that aren't data.

Scenario C — The franken-deck (masters & layouts)

The range-review deck was assembled from three older decks: three slide masters aboard, four title styles, footers that disagree about the date, and a logo that jumps 5mm between slides.

A correct consolidation: one master, imported slides re-homed onto its layouts (Reset, then pick the right layout — not hand-fixing each slide) · title/footer/logo corrections made ONCE on the master, landing everywhere · text living in placeholders, not free-floating text boxes · the learner can say which fix went to the master, which to a layout, and which (rarely) to a slide.

Scenario D — The template job (reuse)

A new supplier-day deck is needed by Thursday. The corporate template exists. The usual move — copying last quarter's deck and overtyping — is how the franken-deck (Scenario C) was born.

A correct start: new deck born from the template (New → from template, not Save-As on an old deck) · the template's layouts used as designed — section header for sections, content layouts for content, no hand-drawn title boxes · theme colours/fonts untouched (if it needs a colour the theme lacks, that's a brand question, not a formatting one) · reused slides brought in with destination formatting so they adopt the template.

Scenario E — The 10am presentation (present & share)

Tomorrow, 10am, the GDM room: your range review, 15 minutes, then the deck goes to attendees and two people who weren't there.

A correct last mile: rehearsed once in presenter view (notes as cues, next-slide preview, timer) with timings checked against the slot · the deck that travels is an export, not the working file: PDF for fidelity to externals, notes pages for the two absentees · fonts/media checked on a machine that isn't yours (or PDF'd so it can't matter) · file named so the receiver can file it — not "final_v3_FINAL".
Make it yours. These are fictional. Describe your real deck disasters and the lessons retarget to your actual template and review cycle.
