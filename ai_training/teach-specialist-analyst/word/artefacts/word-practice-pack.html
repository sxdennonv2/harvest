The document pack

Scenarios for practising document machinery. The recurring specimen is the range-review submission — a GDM-style document with fictional suppliers shared across the course family. Each scenario describes its specimen precisely enough to reconstruct; rung 4 of the relevant lessons builds the specimens from scratch. Each states what a correct result must contain.

Scenario A — The hand-formatted submission (styles)

The Q3 range-review submission, 14 pages, formatted entirely by hand.

Specimen spec: · "Headings" are Normal text made bold 14pt (sometimes 13.5pt) by selection · Sub-headings: bold + italic, except section 4 where they're underlined · The nav pane shows nothing; outline view is a wall of Normal · One paragraph of body text is accidentally bold 14pt too — it looks like a heading
A correct rescue: real styles applied (Heading 1/2, Body) via select-and-click, not retyped · the fake heading demoted by style, not by unbolding · nav pane now walks the document · one global change (Heading 2 colour) made ONCE in the style and landing everywhere · zero direct character formatting doing a style's job.

Scenario B — The fighting page numbers (structure)

Same submission: the title page shows "Page 1", the annex restarts numbering randomly, landscape tables flipped the whole document sideways once, and the table of contents was typed by hand in March — it lies.

A correct structure: section breaks placed deliberately (title page unnumbered via different-first-page/section; annex restarting at 1 by decision, not accident; landscape confined to its own section) · the typed TOC deleted and replaced by a real one generated from the heading styles · the test: add a heading, update the field, the TOC follows · page breaks by Ctrl+Enter, never Enter-Enter-Enter.

Scenario C — The drifting table (tables & figures)

The submission's supplier-comparison table: five suppliers × six criteria, plus one chart pasted from the course workbook.

Specimen spec: · Column widths drift every time someone types a long supplier name · The header row vanishes when the table crosses to page 7 · Two "columns" in the summary below it are actually tab-spaced text · The chart has floated to the wrong page; its caption was typed as plain text
A correct fix: fixed column widths (AutoFit off by decision) · header row set to repeat across pages · the tab-spaced fake columns rebuilt as a real table · the chart anchored to its paragraph deliberately, with a real caption (Insert Caption) so a table-of-figures could build itself · the test: type a long name, nothing drifts.

Scenario D — The reviewed draft (review & collaborate)

The submission comes back from two reviewers: your director (tracked changes: tightened the exec summary, changed a volume figure 110 → 100, deleted a paragraph) and Merch (five comments: two questions, two style quibbles, one "check this with Supply Chain").

A correct review pass: markup views used deliberately (Simple Markup to read, All Markup to decide) · the volume change 110 → 100 NOT accepted silently — it's a fact change, verified against the source before accept/reject (the proof-pass discipline) · prose tightening accepted or rejected per change, not Accept All · every comment resolved or answered, the check-with-Supply-Chain one spawning a real follow-up · final saved WITHOUT tracked history riding along.

Scenario E — The new-doc job (templates & consistency)

A supplier-onboarding pack is needed. The org template exists (cover, styled headings, footer with doc control). The usual move — Save-As on the last onboarding pack — is why three live documents still carry a 2024 footer and Riverside's name in the metadata.

A correct start: New → from the template, never Save-As on a predecessor · the template's styles used as given (if a style is missing, that's a template-owner request, not a local invention) · standing clauses inserted as building blocks (Quick Parts — the same instrument Outlook taught), not retyped from memory · document properties/metadata clean — the test: Inspect Document finds no previous supplier's ghost.
