# Outlook to a High Standard — Resources

Curated, high-trust sources. Knowledge for lessons is drawn from here, not from guesses.

> Note: links are the canonical, long-stable URLs for each source. Verify/trim on next
> online session (same seeding convention as the Excel course).

## Knowledge

- [Microsoft Support — Outlook help & learning](https://support.microsoft.com/outlook)
  Authoritative primary source for feature behaviour across new/classic Outlook. Use
  for: rules, categories, Quick Steps, search, calendar features, what changed.
- [Microsoft — Search email in Outlook (search operators)](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/search-email-in-outlook-7d060fe6-eea4-4a91-96f8-2c6c4c209513)
  The canonical reference for from:, subject:, hasattachments:, received: and friends.
  Use for: exact operator syntax and combining criteria.
- [Microsoft — Manage email messages by using rules](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/manage-email-messages-by-using-rules-in-outlook-c24f5dea-9465-4df4-ad17-a50704d66c59)
  Use for: rule conditions/actions/ordering, client vs server rules.
- [Microsoft — Automate common or repetitive tasks with Quick Steps](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/automate-common-or-repetitive-tasks-with-quick-steps-in-outlook-b184f89f-3738-4562-96de-c0244ea830f2)
  Use for: what Quick Steps can chain, and their limits vs rules.
- [Microsoft — Copilot in Outlook](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot-outlook)
  Use for: what Copilot actually does in Outlook (summarise, draft, coaching) and what
  it can't (create rules, folders) — keeps rung 3 honest.
