Projection 4 · meal selection mechanism

From judgement call to a mechanism

The concrete design that replaces "does Debbie judge this house compatible?" with "do these residents' option pools happen to overlap?" — sketch only, nothing built yet.

StepWhat it doesStatus
1 · Option pool Each resident gets up to 10 named options per meal slot, already assumed compatible with their constraint-block (dislikes, portion-guide, etc.). Debbie builds these quarterly; each pool version is valid between a start/end date, and options can be edited mid-quarter, effective immediately Cadence/validity confirmed — no real pools populated for any resident yet
2 · Allocation Random: uniform pick from the currently-valid pool. Stratified random: stratifies by variety across the 28-day period — shuffled rounds through the pool so each option is used a roughly even number of times, not clustered repeats. Manual override: Debbie can change as much or as little as she wants, cell by cell, independently — no cascading logic All three modes fully specified
3 · Harmonization Check whether an option appears in multiple residents' currently-valid pools for the same house/day/slot; if so, assign that same option to all of them — one cook, several residents. Scoped to one residence, never crosses houses Sketch only — this is the mechanical answer to the dinner-sharing question above
4 · Savings tracking Count harmonized day/slot combinations and how many residents each covered, so the time/cost benefit is measured, not assumed Metric sketch only — no real numbers yet
5 · Review interface Every resident × meal-slot × day cell renders as a dropdown of that resident's option pool, pre-filled by steps 2–3's output, in a web/HTML UI. Debbie changes as much or as little as she wants, cell by cell Fully specified
6 · Plan lifecycle draft ↔ locked (Debbie's explicit action, reversible directly; hard gate — cannot shop until locked) → shopped (reversible — can reset to draft, clearing the shopping list; hard gate — cannot close until shopped) → closed (true terminal — no further changes, ever) Fully resolved: two paths back to draft (direct unlock, or via shopped); closed is the only one-way door

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (first pass)

Dinner-sharing is conditional, not universal — a house can share one dinner across residents only when their dietary requirements are compatible; where needs clash, each resident needs their own. Roster is volatile — support workers can be swapped at short notice.

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (second pass)

Roster parked — kept as an axis, fixed to one placeholder value (TBA); not pursued as live data for now. Meal-selection mechanism adopted — up to 10 candidate options per resident per meal slot, random baseline allocation, and harmonization (overlap-detection across a house's residents) as the mechanical answer to dinner-sharing, with savings tracked rather than assumed. See the mechanism.

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (third pass)

Who builds the pool, and when — Debbie, quarterly (4×/year). Validity — each pool version applies between a start/end date; a resident has a sequence of pool versions over time. Mid-quarter edits — options can be changed anytime, taking effect immediately until altered again. Allocation modes — random, stratified random, and manual override are all confirmed available; Debbie can override any automated pick.

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (fourth pass)

Stratification variable chosen — variety across the 28-day period. Stratified-random works through a resident's pool in shuffled rounds so each option gets used a roughly even number of times, rather than a plain random draw that could by chance cluster repeats. Not zero repetition — a 10-option pool over 28 days still repeats each option ~2-3 times — just no clustering.

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (fifth pass)

Review interface confirmed — every resident × meal-slot × day cell is a dropdown of that resident's option pool. The algorithm pre-fills each cell; Debbie reviews and changes any cell directly. This turns "Debbie can override any pick" into a concrete UI: the dropdown itself is the override mechanism, not a separate control.

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (sixth pass)

Override scope — Debbie can change as much or as little as she wants, cell by cell, independently; no automatic re-harmonization runs. Artifact format — a web/HTML UI hosts the review step. Plan lifecycle introduced — draft → locked → shopping_list_generated (later revised, see eighth pass below — this pass's terminal-state claim was wrong).

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (seventh pass)

Both lifecycle gates confirmed as hard rules, not just descriptions of a typical path: a plan cannot be used to generate a shopping list until it is locked (no exceptions), and a shopped plan cannot be changed further (no exceptions). The one remaining gap: whether locked is itself reversible before shopping happens.

Revised · 05 Jul 2026 (eighth pass)

Correction: the seventh pass's claim that a shopped plan "cannot be changed further" was wrong. There is a fourth state: draft → locked → shopped → closed. shopped is reversible — it can reset back to draft, clearing the generated shopping list. closed is the true terminal state, reached only after shopping (another hard gate) — and only closed is confirmed unconditionally immutable.

Resolved · 05 Jul 2026 (ninth pass)

Lifecycle fully specified — locked can revert directly back to draft (an unlock), confirmed to not require going via shopped. The plan lifecycle now has zero open questions: two paths lead back to draft (direct unlock from locked, or reset from shopped); closed is the sole one-way door.
